FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Yes, window tinting is legal in Victoria. Front side windows must allow at least 35% VLT. Rear windows (behind the driver) can be any darkness provided the vehicle has working rear-view mirrors. Windscreens can have a clear UV film or a light tint in the top visor strip. We ensure all our installs are road legal and compliant with Victorian road rules.

Most standard vehicles (hatchbacks, sedans) take 2–3 hours. SUVs and wagons typically take 3–4 hours. Large vans or vehicles requiring old tint removal may take longer. Same-day appointments are usually available. We recommend dropping your car off in the morning for same-day collection.

We recommend waiting at least 3–5 days before rolling your windows down to allow the adhesive to fully cure. During curing, you may notice small water bubbles or hazy areas – these are completely normal and will disappear as the film dries. Avoid washing your car for the first 5–7 days.

Standard dyed films have minimal impact on signals. Our Carbon and Ceramic films are completely free of metallic components, meaning zero signal interference for GPS, mobile phones, Bluetooth, E-Tag, or satellite radio. You can enjoy all the heat rejection benefits without compromising connectivity.

Carbon tint uses carbon particles to block IR heat and UV rays – it's an excellent mid-range option with no signal interference and 10-year warranty. Ceramic tint uses nano-ceramic particles to deliver the highest performance: up to 80% heat rejection, 99% UV blocking, crystal-clear visibility, and a lifetime warranty. Ceramic is the premium choice for maximum comfort.

Yes! All our films come with a manufacturer-backed warranty: Standard film – 5 years, Carbon film – 10 years, Ceramic film – Lifetime. Our warranty covers peeling, bubbling, cracking, and colour fading. If any issue arises with the film (not due to physical damage), we'll repair or replace it at no charge.
